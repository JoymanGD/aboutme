So!

Recently while thinking of my "Legends of the Old Universe" I came up with the idea of scaling different lores/universes/worlds based on their life intensity and realism (lets call it LIS - life intensity scale).

What does it mean you will ask? It means how far is their everyday life from our real everyday life.

Why do I need it you will ask? So the world could be built consistently. You have your own world image in your head, so you save this image by describing it's properties, you develop it according to those properties, and finally you get consistent world that people will believe in. And one of such properties is LIS.

Heres an example so you could understand it better:
- Real life = 0.
- Game of Thrones = 6. Yes, there are Winter, dragons and magic. I know. But also gross, harsh and hard everyday medieval life is well shown there. Gods are never shown, we can just hear prayers to them. So let it be 6 for illustration.
- LOTR = 8. Much more magic, dragons, orc, trolles and gods.
- Warhammer 40k = 10. It could be 40k instead of 10 because of it's epicness but lets limit the scale by 10.

Another question: what level do I want my world to be on? From the very first day I had this vision or, better to say, feeling of how it should be. The problem is that it's impossible to make your fantasy story realistic AND breathtaking at the same time. But you still want people to believe in it so it should also be realistic in a sense. 
I don't really want Old Universe to feel very different during it's development but still need to keep it progressing. So I'd scale it dynamically but carefully.

(btw by development I mean mostly how people are getting familiar with the universe and lore)

1. Begining of it's development - I'd give it 2-3. Most of the time you should see everyday life of a medieval people, nice landscapes, no magic. AND sometimes you see creatures like Kratt - a magical creature BUT is used for help in household chores. This detail is kinda keeping it more grounded, less epic.
2. Then it's 3-4. So you can witness that there is magicable magic. People can use it, but not that drastically - for that in Ursia, for example, most of the people cant neither use nor feel magic. Some people can feel it, and only very few can use it.
3. Then you should get familiar with gods, but in the first story I am writing gods are just energy sources  (stored in artifacts) and only really few of them are mentioned. So I'd like to keep it near 4-5.

So this is today's LIS of the Old Universe I have in my head. Interesting to see if it is going to change with time.

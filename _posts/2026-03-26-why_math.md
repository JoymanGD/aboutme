So!

I started getting interested in math as soon as I started working as a game developer. Usually for the most gamedev tasks all you need to know is linear algebra and trigonometry. Sometimes you can face a bit more advanced math like sets, derivatives, integrals, gradients etc.

I think my first math wall was Fourier transforms - I was about to implement a FFT (Fast Fourier Transform) ocean which took me really long. I know some people that go smoothly through such topics but that's not me. I spent too much time because it was my first time reading [some really mathy paper](https://jtessen.people.clemson.edu/reports/papers_files/coursenotes2004.pdf). Understanding the topic was a huge win for me and that's basically what made me buy a few books and continue studying math.

So this section of my personal blog is my math hideout where I first don't understand but then I [also don't understand](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dQw4w9WgXcQ), but finally I might understand something. And thats why I'm trying to evolve here!
